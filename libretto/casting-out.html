The Casting Out

(in the meter of an Italian Sonnet, i.e. ABBA ABBA CDECDE, until the final sonnet, where the sestet shifts to CDCCDC)

The Community
What sins did blind your eyes we cannot know
a baby in the womb seems free of craft
your mother is well known here, yea, beloved
your father lives the Law not just for show
Our hearts rejoice: our sister found her sight
beholds, espies, and looks and views and sees
God's stars above, the fish within the seas
the trees and birds brought forth by morning's light.
But trust we must what Moses prophesized.
Which other prophet could we ever need
than him who led our ancestors to here?
The Nazarene these covenants defies
a Sabbath healing--what a brazen deed!
He topples all that we here hold most dear.
The Woman
My eyes were blind since birth, you all agree
No fasting, praying that we haven't tried
My pilgrimages skipped no holy site
There seemed no hope for me to ever see.
Oh thirty years in darkness is so long
and fifteen years of begging at the gate
just sitting in the dust from dawn till late
I praise the Lord he made my heart so strong
The same Lord gave the Nazarene his power
whose light broke through my vision's pitch black cloud
For whence else could such awesome power be?
I speak of opened eyes, you of the hour
and day on which with sight I was endowed.
Yes, Sabbath! Still: God harkened Jesus' plea!
The Community
What sins did blind your eyes we cannot know
but sin was there, besmirching you like mud
it made you ugly weed, not blooming bud!
And now you lecture us on God--how so?
Yes, God will hear a righteous heart's strong plea,
but first the righteous heart harkens to God
not tread upon the Sabbath's Law like sod!
What power could such novel rites decree?
Be his disciple, we take Moses' side.
Our comradship with you and yours is through.
God showed you grace and you will not abide
by celebrating Sabbath true and tried?
Go now, we hear your "prophet" calling you.
They claim that Jesus said: Hell's road is wide.
