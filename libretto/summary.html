Summary of John 9,1pp -- A Gospel Cantata

As the sun rises on the day of the Sabbath, the people rejoice in the way that the Justice of the Lord presents itself with the sunrise, as the sun reveals all iniquities.

Meanwhile, Jesus and his disciples encounter a beggar woman at one of the city gates of Jerusalem who was born blind. The disciples wish to know how the Justice of the Lord reveals herself in the blind-born woman: is it her own sin, or her parents, that made her thus?

Jesus denies both interpretations. This woman is a sign for the world of the coming light.

Jesus speaks to the woman. She tells him that she was born blind and that she desired two things most in the world -- to see her husband's eyes and her children's smiles.

Jesus sends her to the Shiloh pond, telling her that it will heal her; she refuses to go, because she does not believe anything will happen. Jesus then puts mud on her eyes and asks her to go wash it off; this time she has little choice and oblieges him.

At the pond, her family sees her wash off the mud and watches in amazement as she can finally see: her parents, her husband's eyes, her children smiling. They praise God together. The parents are relieved that it was not their sin that caused the blindness.
