Summary of John 9,1pp -- A Gospel Cantata

(Dramatis Personae)

Opening Scene (Chorus)

After a reminder of the duties of the Sabbath and its many rules, the sun rises (at the midpoint of the Sabbath). The people of Jerusalem rejoice in the way that the Justice of the Lord presents itself with the sunrise and reveals all iniquities.

And they know which iniquities these are, because in the Mosaic Law they have the enumeration of their behavior, and those who follow it fare well. To them, the sun's light reveals their rewards: the city of Jerusalem, their spouses and their offsprings.

Recitativo: The Sending (Jesus, Disciples, Woman)

Meanwhile, Jesus and his disciples encounter a beggar woman at one of the city gates of Jerusalem who was born blind. The disciples discuss how the Justice of the Lord reveals herself in the blind-born woman: is it her own sin, or her parents, that made her thus?

Jesus denies both interpretations. This woman is a sign for the world of the coming light.

Jesus speaks to the woman and sends her to the Shiloh pond to wash her eyes. She explains that they are not dirty, but that she is blind since birth. Much to the disciples consternation, Jesus then puts mud on her eyes and asks her to go wash it off; this time she has little choice and oblieges him.

Aria: Anger and Healing (Woman)

The beggar woman complains bitterly about having to walk to Shiloh, about losing her spot at the gate, about the cleverness of the "teacher"--pun about "sending" her to "Shiloh", how a teacher should not be picking on the handicapped, etc. But as she washes the mud off, the music transforms into a reprise of the Opening Scene, and now she too can see Jerusalem and her family, who are rushing to her side.

The Reaction of the Religious Estabishment

The Reaction of the Community

The Woman Leaves the Community (Aria)

Jesus returns for her Sending (Duet)

The Woman now understands that by gaining sight, she bargained for more insight into the inquities of the world than she understood. She now sees clearly the shortcomings of the establishment and she can not turn a blind eye to them.

Jesus agrees with her using quotes from the current and other parts of the Gospel of John:

  • John 9:39 For judgment I have come into this world, so that the blind will see and those who see will become blind.
  • John 12:46 I have come into the world as a light, so that no one who believes in me should stay in darkness.
  • John 8:12 I am the light of the world. Whoever follows me will never walk in darkness, but will have the light of life.
  • John 7:7 The world cannot hate you, but it hates me because I testify that what it does is evil.

Closing Chorus: Light of the World
